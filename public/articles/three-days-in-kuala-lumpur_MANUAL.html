Three Days in Kuala Lumpur

How to spend three days in the city

#kualalumpur

#malaysia

#travel

I recently had the pleasure of visiting Kuala Lumpur in Malaysia. Here's how I spent my three days in the city! If you want to read about my first impression of KL, you can do that here.
My itinerary was pretty relaxed because I also spent a lot of time working on a project, so you could probably fit more things into three days. But here's what I did:

Day 1: Arriving & Petronas Towers

Getting to the city

Most people will probably arrive at Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA, KUL) which is the biggest airport in KL. There are two main options to get to the city:

  • By car - Taxi or Grab - 1h (RM 65)
  • Public transport: KLIA Transit - 1h 25 to KL Sentral (RM 55)

I took a taxi to my hotel, the Sheraton Imperial. The main reason was that the JW Marriott, which would've been my first choice, didn't have any availabilty on the first night. The Sheraton was very good too, it was recently renovated and their Club Lounge is great.

Merdeka 118 from the Sheraton Club Lounge
KL Tower

Petronas Towers / KLCC

After getting some food I took a Grab to KLCC Park, and decided to walk to the Petronas Towers. It's not that big, around 1/3 the size of Bangkok's Lumphini Park. It's calm to walk around though, and there is even a jogging track if you can deal with the heat. It's pretty dark though at night.

There are a couple of things to do around the KLCC area:

  • Petronas Towers: KL's iconic twin towers. They're lit up at night, and there is an observation deck (09:00 - 21:00). A reservation is required though, and they're usually sold out a few days before.
  • Suria KLCC, a giant shopping mall with tons of shopping options
  • The SkyBar at the Traders Hotel for amazing views of the Petronas Towers

KLCC is also close to Saloma Link, a popular bridge that lights up in different colors at night.

Day 2: Exploring & KL Tower

On the second day, I spent some time exploring the city. I started off at Independence Square, where the Sultan Abdul Samad Building is located. It was home to numerous British agencies during the British rule, and currently houses some ministries of the Malaysian government. I also walked around the Sri Mahamariamman Temple, another one of KL's recognizable spots.

The Sultan Abdul Samad Building
Sri Mahamariamman Temple

But the higlight was definitely my visit to KL Tower. I'ts 421 meters tall, which makes it the world's 7th tallest tower. There are multiple observation decks with an amazing view, and there is also a revolving restaurant on one of the upper floors. You can see both the Petronas Towers / KLCC, as well as the new mega-tall Merdeka 118 skyscraper (the world's second largest building). Absolutely fantastic!

KLCC & Petronas Towers
Merdeka 118

I highly recommend a visit to KL Tower and one of its observation decks. Tickets are usually easier to get than for the Petronas Towers and the view is amazing!

Day 3: Batu Caves

On the second day, I first went to walk around the Perdana Botanical Garden. It's a huge park with small lakes, fountains and lots of plants of course! You can walk around and explore the many attractivions like a deer park, the Hibiscus and the Orchid garden. There is also a butterfly park.

Perdana Botanical Gardens (1)
Perdana Botanical Gardens (2)

I then took a Grab to another famous KL attraction: The Batu Caves! And I was not disappointed - they look absolutely stunning. Go there during or right before sunset, there'll be less people and the soft light makes everything look nice.

Stairs
Entrance to the caves

If you want to get to the entrance, you'll need to walk up a few stairs. It's pretty steep but fun, and there are some funny monkeys along the way too. I didn't actually go inside the caves because I was super hungry - there is a guy selling ice cream and drinks up there if you need some sugar before walking back down.

That's it - this are some of the things I did during my three days online. If you get bored, here are some more recommendations: Jalan Alor for food and Pavilion & The Gardens Mall for shopping. Or go have a look at one of the many mosques in KL!
